title: "Failure-Mode-Specific Bond-Strength Equations for Reinforced Concrete Using SHAP-Guided Machine Learning"
excerpt: "This project develops explicit, hand-calculable equations for the bond strength between deformed steel bars and concrete, treating pullout and splitting failures separately rather than averaging over two distinct mechanisms. Drawing on a compiled database of 519 pull-out tests, gradient-boosting and XGBoost models are trained as high-accuracy teacher models, and SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) is then used to reveal how each variable — concrete strength, yield strength, cover, anchorage length, and bar diameter — drives bond capacity. These trends are captured as bounded closed-form functions that become the terms of the final equations. The entire pipeline is rebuilt inside repeated nested cross-validation, so the reported accuracy reflects genuinely unseen data, while bootstrap resampling quantifies the uncertainty in every coefficient. The result is a set of transparent equations that retain most of the predictive power of the machine-learning models while remaining simple enough to evaluate by hand. The manuscript is currently being prepared for journal submission."
